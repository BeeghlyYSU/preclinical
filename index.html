Welcome to the Preclinical Field Experience

The Beeghly College of Liberal Arts, Social Sciences and Education’s required preclinical experience plays a significant role in the development of teacher education candidates as teachers. The preclinical field-based experience requires a substantial time commitment, as candidates spend time in an assigned school district. The nature and duration of field-based activities vary in the amount of observation and participation based on specific course and program requirements. Candidates are generally enrolled in the preclinical experience the semester prior to student teaching for the experience is a preparation for student teaching.

The preclinical experience provides candidates the opportunity to explore and develop their own teaching philosophy, work in the classroom with students, and grow professionally as a result of collaborating with teaching professionals. Candidates learn about teaching style, content, classroom management and discipline through developing and teaching lessons as a part of an instructional unit that are aligned with professional and state academic content standards. Resource teachers and university supervisors play an integral part in candidates’ professional development through mentoring and feedback.

In order to participate in the preclinical experience, all preclinical candidates are screened for eligibility for specific course and grade point average prerequisites must be met. Then, at the conclusion of the experience, candidates’ preclinical development is reviewed for a determination of preparedness for the student teaching experience.

We acknowledge and thank our school district partners for their commitment to providing instructional opportunities and mentoring during this important experience which guides our teacher candidates towards successful preclinical practice and meaningful careers.
